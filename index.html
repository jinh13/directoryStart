FOOD | Explore St. Louis!

Ever had a hard time finding the best restaurants for breakfast (includes brunch of course!), lunch, or dinner? Look no further, here is a list of amazing restaurants, broken down by category and price points!

Meet Heather!

Heather is a student at Washington University in St. Louis who has spent just as much time exploring food joints in St. Louis than in the classroom.

Breakfast

Name Price Review
Half & Half Price: $ Located in Clayton, Half & Half is a bustling breakfast joint with adorable blue accents. The best thing at Half & Half are the vegetable hash browns -- they come out steaming hot and the brussel sprouts are cooked perfectly! They also serve breakfast for dinner so it's perfect for those nights you're just craving pancakes.
Rooster Price: $$ There are two location, one in Downtown and the other on South Grand. Rooster is famous for their mimosas and just stunning sausage# 2 crepes (my personal favorite crepe ever!). Don't forget their blueberry pancakes and the classic St. Louis slinger!
Winslow's Home Price: $$ Winslow's is a huge hit for WashU students because it's just so close to campus! They have a wonderful outdoor patio that is just perfect for sunny Sunday mornings. Their quiche and salmon is to die for, and everything in their bakery is delicious.

Lunch

Name Price Review
Crushed Red Price: $ Crushed Red is a great spot if you want to grab something quick during your lunch break! It's quick but it's still so delicious -- you can build your own salad or pizza or you can pick one of the delicious already-created options, as well. There are so many options at Crushed Red, no one would ever get bored here!
Thai 202 Price: $$ Thai 202 is a tiny little restaurant in the beautiful Central West End with dishes packing big flavor. They serve all the classic Thai dishes such as pad thai and curries but try out their more creative dishes like the healthy rice (trust me it tastes better than it sounds) and fried sweet potatoes!
Seoul Taco Price: $ Seoul Taco has truly created a name for themselves. Started as a food truck, Seoul Taco has expanded to have permanent store front. They have a very short menu but every item is so flavorful and the fusion between Korean and Mexican food is just genius. Quick, cheap, and delicious! It's a no brainer!

Dinner

Name Price Review
Brasserie by Niche Price: $$$ Brasserie is my favorite restaurant in all of St. Louis. A gorgeous restuarant in the heart of the Central West End, Brasserie is the perfect restaurant for dinners with family. Brasserie is truly authentic French food, and their beef stew and olives are my favorite.
Tani Sushi Bistro Price: $$$ Tani's is an upscale sushi restaurant in Clayton, and it's perfect for date nights! Their sushi rolls are extravagent, stunning, and absolutely divine. Treat yourself and others to a night here; add a sake bomb if you would like!
Three Kings Public House Price: $$ Do you want a laid-back night surrounded by good friends, good drinks, good food, and good vibes? Three kings is the perfect place! Three Kings has an extensive selection of beer, truly delicious wings and burgers, and the atmosphere here is hard to beat. They do bar food so well, I had to include Three Kings!
